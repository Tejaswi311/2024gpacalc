What is Weighted GPA

Weighted GPA is a method used to calculate a student's grade point average by giving additional value to challenging courses like honors, AP, or IB classes. Unlike the standard unweighted GPA, which treats all classes equally, weighted GPA considers the difficulty level of each course.Regular vs. Advanced Classes: Regular classes follow the typical grading scale (A=4.0, B=3.0), while advanced courses receive extra weight. For example, an A in an honors or AP/IB class might be rated higher than 4.0.Calculation: Weighted GPA combines grades from all classes, multiplying advanced course grades by their weighted value. The total weighted grade points are added to the unweighted GPA to determine the final weighted GPA.Purpose: Weighted GPAs acknowledge students who take challenging courses by rewarding them with higher GPAs. This system incentivizes students to pursue more rigorous academic paths.Variations: Weighted GPA systems differ among schools. Grading scales and weights assigned to advanced classes vary based on each institution's policies.Overall, weighted GPAs aim to recognize students' efforts in pursuing academic challenges, providing them with higher GPAs compared to those solely enrolled in standard-level classes, even with similar grades.
