What is Unweighted GPA

The Unweighted GPA calculation involves converting each received letter grade into its corresponding numerical value and then taking the average of those values. This process does not consider the class's difficulty level, focusing solely on the achieved letter grades. Therefore, a higher Unweighted GPA indicates consistent academic effort and success across different subjects, demonstrating a commitment to academic excellence and the ability to handle college-level coursework. Admissions officers often use the Unweighted GPA as a significant indicator of a student's dedication to academics and their potential to excel in higher education.
