Instructions: How to use

This is specifically created for students in college in general, you school might do it differently so please be cautious. To use the calculator: Type in the name of the class you are taking and the grade you received in that class and the credit hours using the dropdowns. To add the class to the table press add class. In the table it will show the name of the class you took, the grade you received, the corresponding grade points, credit hours and the total points for that class. At the bottom of the chart your GPA will show, it will be updating as you add values to the chart.

What is College GPA

This calculator only provides the weighted college calculation unweighted stays the same in both college and high school.A college GPA is a numeric summary of a student's academic performance in college. It's derived by converting letter grades or percentages to numerical values, then averaging them for an overall score. Grades are often assigned numerical equivalents (e.g., A = 4.0, B = 3.0). Each course's grade is multiplied by its credit hours to obtain grade points. The sum of grade points across all courses is divided by the total credit hours to determine the GPA. GPA scales typically range from 0.0 to 4.0 or 5.0, varying by institution. A higher GPA reflects stronger academic performance. Colleges use GPA for admissions, scholarships, and evaluating a student's progress.
